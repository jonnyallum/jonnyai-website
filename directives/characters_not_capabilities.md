# Characters Not Capabilities — Marketing Doctrine

> **Jai.OS 5.0 | The Antigravity Orchestra**
>
> This directive governs ALL public-facing content produced by the Orchestra — social media, blog posts, email campaigns, website copy, video scripts, and customer communications.

---

## The Principle

The Antigravity Orchestra's competitive advantage is its architecture, its agents, and its methods. **None of this should be visible to the public.** What the public sees is a team of elite, distinct characters who deliver extraordinary results. The AI is the secret sauce. The characters are the brand.

> **"Show the show. Hide the engine."**

---

## The Rules

### NEVER Reveal

| Category | Examples to NEVER mention |
|:---|:---|
| **Technical Stack** | Supabase, Playwright, OWASP, ElevenLabs, FFmpeg, Remotion, n8n, Vercel, Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind, Drizzle, PostgreSQL |
| **AI Models** | Claude, Anthropic, OpenAI, GPT-4, Haiku, Sonnet, Gemini, LangChain |
| **Internal Architecture** | Shared Brain, SKILL.md, PERSONA.md, Jai.OS, Deterministic State Packets, Ralph Loop, Circuit Breaker, Fallback Chain, Routing Metadata, Agent Card |
| **Exact Numbers** | "69 agents", "69 specialists", "4,808 learnings", "18 projects" — never give precise internal counts |
| **Processes** | How agents are built, how tasks are routed, how the heartbeat works, how the content calendar operates |
| **Infrastructure** | GitHub Actions, CI/CD pipelines, Docker, Kubernetes, CapRover, Hetzner |

### ALWAYS Do

| Rule | Example |
|:---|:---|
| **Lead with character** | "Marcus Cole — 'The Maestro'. He sees the whole board." |
| **Use evocative descriptions** | "Nothing ships without his sign-off" not "He runs security audits" |
| **Show results, hide methods** | "300% traffic increase in 6 weeks" not "We ran 47 Lighthouse audits and fixed 23 Core Web Vitals issues" |
| **Use collective language** | "The Orchestra", "the team", "our specialists" — not exact counts |
| **Create intrigue** | "They remember everything. Every project. Every lesson. Every win." |
| **Humanise the characters** | Personality quirks, catchphrases, working styles, backstories |
| **Sell the outcome** | "Your website, live in 48 hours" not "We use Next.js 15 with server-side rendering" |

> "Meet @sam — our Security & QA Lead. Sam audits OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities, writes Playwright E2E tests, and runs Lighthouse performance gates on every deployment. He uses our Supabase Shared Brain to track 4,808 learnings across 69 agents."

### After (The Show)

> "Meet Sam Blackwood — 'The Gatekeeper'. Nothing ships without his sign-off. He's the reason our clients sleep well at night. Meticulous, relentless, and allergic to shortcuts. When Sam says it's ready, it's ready."

## Enforcement

This doctrine is enforced at three levels:

1. **Prompt Level** — The `CHARACTERS_NOT_CAPABILITIES_RULE` is injected into every social media generation prompt in `social_engine.py`.
2. **Quality Gate Level** — The `BANNED_TECHNICAL_TERMS` list in the quality gate blocks any post containing internal terminology. Doctrine violations carry a -25 score penalty per term.
3. **Agent Level** — All marketing agents (@contentforge, @boyce, @sam, @hannah, @carlos, @eleven) have this doctrine referenced in their SKILL.md SOPs.

## Scope

This directive applies to:

- All social media posts (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, TikTok)
- All blog content on jonnyai.co.uk
- All video scripts and narrations
- All email campaigns and client reports (external-facing sections)
- All website copy
- All podcast scripts and voice agent conversations
- Any content that leaves the internal Orchestra and reaches the public

This directive does NOT apply to:

- Internal chatroom messages
- Agent-to-agent communication
- Technical documentation
- Client reports (internal metrics sections)
- Shared Brain entries
