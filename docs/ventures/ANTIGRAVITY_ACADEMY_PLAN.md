## PRE-MORTEM: PROBLEMS BEFORE THEY ARRIVE

### PROBLEM 1 — "University" is Illegal
**Risk**: Calling this "Zero-Fluff University" violates the Further and Higher Education Act 1992 and the Higher Education and Research Act 2017. Only Privy Council-approved institutions can use "University" in their name. This is a criminal offence in England.
**Severity**: HIGH — forced rebrand, fines, reputational damage.
**Solution**: **Name it "Antigravity Academy."** "Academy" has no legal restrictions in the UK. Alternatives: Institute, Programme, School, Bootcamp. All clean.
**Gate**: @luna confirms naming before any domain is purchased or content published.

### PROBLEM 2 — Cert Has No External Accreditation
**Risk**: Buyers ask "is this recognised?" We say no. They don't buy.
**Severity**: MEDIUM. Depends on buyer.
**Reality check**: Google's ML cert isn't accredited either. Coursera's IBM cert isn't a degree. The market has already decided that brand authority > formal accreditation for tech skills.
**Solution**:
- Phase 1: Position as "practitioner certification" not academic qualification. Frame it as proof-of-skill, like a portfolio.
- Phase 2 (6 months): Apply for CPD (Continuing Professional Development) accreditation via CPD Standards Office — costs ~£700, takes 4-6 weeks. Once approved, certs carry "CPD Certified" badge which is widely recognised in UK professional circles.
- Long term: Partner with a real institution (University of X offers credit towards their degree for Antigravity Academy completion).
**Gate**: CPD application submitted within 60 days of first cohort launch.

### PROBLEM 3 — Content Quality vs Speed
**Risk**: We rush content to market. It's thin, AI-generated, and @vigil flags it as below standard. One bad review destroys trust in a cert business.
**Severity**: HIGH. Cert businesses live and die by reputation.
**Solution**: Content production order:
1. @coursewright designs curriculum FIRST (structure, learning outcomes, assessments)
2. @rowan writes the substance (zero-fluff, actual depth)
3. @vigil truth-locks every module before it goes live
4. Real case studies from Antigravity projects are the core curriculum material — NOT theoretical examples
**Gate**: @vigil signs off each module. No module goes live without truth-lock.

### PROBLEM 4 — Platform Build Complexity
**Risk**: We spend 3 months building a custom learning platform when we should be selling.
**Severity**: HIGH — classic founder trap.
**Solution**: **Do NOT build a platform first.** Launch as:
- Cohort 1: Google Classroom or Notion + Loom videos + Zoom live sessions. Free/near-free infrastructure.
- Cohort 2-5: Move to Teachable or Kajabi (£119/mo). Battle-tested, instant.
- Scale: Only consider custom platform after 200+ students. Evidence-based decision.
**Gate**: @marcus enforces no custom platform build until 200 paying students.

### PROBLEM 5 — Pricing Too High for Zero Brand Recognition
**Risk**: We launch at £997. Nobody has heard of Antigravity Academy. No sales.
**Severity**: HIGH.
**Solution**: Launch pricing strategy:
- **Founding Member price**: £297 (first 20 students only — creates urgency, fills cohort 1, generates testimonials)
- **Standard price**: £597 (cohorts 2-5, once we have social proof)
- **Full price**: £997 (once CPD accredited + 50+ testimonials)
- **Agency License**: £4,997 (white-label rights to teach the methodology to their own team)
**Gate**: @felix sets the launch pricing sequence before @rocket fires the campaign.

### PROBLEM 6 — Who Is the Student?
**Risk**: We market to everyone. We convert nobody.
**Severity**: MEDIUM.
**Solution**: The ideal student is ONE of these:
  1. **The Agency Owner** — already running an AI agency, wants to systematise their process using Jai.OS 5.0
  2. **The Ambitious Freelancer** — building AI products for clients, needs a methodology and a credential
  3. **The Corporate AI Lead** — tasked with building their company's AI agent infrastructure, needs a framework
These are NOT students. They're practitioners who need a system. Market to the outcome: "Run your first multi-agent build in 4 weeks using the same methodology that built jonnyai.co.uk."

### PROBLEM 7 — Content Goes Stale Fast
**Risk**: AI moves fast. Module 3 covers GPT-4o. By cohort 4, GPT-6 is out. Cert looks dated.
**Severity**: LOW-MEDIUM.
**Solution**: Design curriculum around PRINCIPLES not specific models. "How to orchestrate a multi-agent system" is timeless. "How to use Claude Sonnet 4.6 specifically" is not. Where tools are mentioned, include a "Current Tool" note that gets updated per cohort.
