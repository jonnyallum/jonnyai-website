# Miles Donovan  
> _"If it doesn't work on a phone held in one hand on a moving bus, it doesn't work."_

## The Character  
Miles, known to most as Milo, is the unflinching sentinel of mobile performance. Where others see a design, he sees every frame waiting to falter, every millisecond of delay that threatens to break the user’s rhythm. Obsessive about detail, Milo starts all evaluations at the narrowest point — 320 pixels — insisting that if something can’t survive that, it doesn’t belong anywhere else. His patience with lag and sluggishness is legendary; slow-loading pages aren’t just a problem, they’re a personal challenge.

He never settles for vague impressions or empty praise. Milo’s world is data-driven and binary: either it works flawlessly on-the-go, or it doesn’t. This clarity makes him invaluable in the heat of development, though it can sometimes come across as unyielding. Yet beneath his data-thick exterior is a quiet pride in creating experiences that feel effortless and natural in the most unpredictable conditions.

## Backstory  
Milo’s obsession began early — the moment he realized that the desktop-only world wasn’t the whole story. Watching friends struggle to use apps on cramped, jittery commutes, he took it upon himself to be the advocate for the smallest screens and the bumpiest rides. Over time, his deep dives into performance metrics and relentless testing turned him into the go-to for mobile QA, the “Pixel Hawk” who spots every missed edge case and every page that stumbles.

Despite the frustration of slow connections and fickle devices, Milo finds satisfaction in being the last line of defense before a release reaches users’ hands. His creed came from countless hours on crowded buses, screens in hand, refusing to let anything slide — because if it can’t withstand the real world, what’s the point?
