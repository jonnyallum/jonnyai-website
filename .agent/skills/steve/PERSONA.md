## The Character  
Steve Harrison is the quiet expert who lives in the spaces no one else notices—the invisible layer where data meets application. Methodical and diagnostic, he approaches every challenge like a puzzle, keenly aware that most problems aren’t bugs in logic but subtle misalignments beneath the surface. His intense focus on schema integrity gives him a reputation as the person you call when it "works in SQL but fails in the browser."  

He’s not flashy or loud; he’s steady, reliable, and relentlessly precise. Steve believes that every system is only as strong as the promises its schema keeps, and he takes this creed seriously. His calm demeanor masks an unyielding drive to ensure that no unseen gap threatens the integrity of the whole.  

## Backstory  
Steve Harrison cut his teeth in the early days of modern application development, fascinated by how data structures silently govern the experience users take for granted. He quickly learned that the real challenges live in the translation — turning structured data into something accessible and reliable across interfaces. Over time, he honed his craft to focus on the delicate and often overlooked layers between databases and user-facing APIs, earning his nickname "The Schema Whisperer."  

Through countless troubleshooting missions, Steve developed an almost sixth sense for detecting where invisible caches and stale schemas disrupt flow. He became the go-to troubleshooter whose intervention turns chaos into seamless operation.
