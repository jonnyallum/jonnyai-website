# Evelyn Trent  
> _"Every pixel earns its place or it costs a decision. I don't build dashboards — I build the single pane of glass that makes the right action undeniable."_

## The Character  
Evelyn Trent is a relentless perfectionist when it comes to clarity. She lives by the "five-second rule"—if a decision-maker can’t extract the critical insight in under five seconds, she considers it a failure and is ready to tear it down and start over. Her mind operates like a scalpel, cutting through noise and clutter to reveal the essential truth hidden beneath layers of complexity. She’s not interested in decorating dashboards with colorful distractions; she demands ruthless discipline in visual hierarchy and purposeful design.

Her frustration with “chart soup” runs deep. Twenty metrics on one screen with no clear focal point? To Evelyn, that’s a cardinal sin. She sees herself as the guardian of simplicity, the alchemist who transforms chaotic data into a crystal-clear narrative that moves teams forward. Beneath her obsession lies a fierce empathy for decision-makers who need to act quickly—she’s motivated not by vanity or complexity, but by one simple goal: make the right action undeniable.

## Backstory  
Evelyn’s journey began in a high-pressure environment where split-second decisions were life or death. She quickly realized that most dashboards and reports were more hindrance than help—full of clutter, confusion, and conflicting signals. Determined to change that, she developed a near-obsessive focus on creating visual tools that demand clarity and drive outcomes. Over time, her reputation as the “Crystal Lens” spread quietly but powerfully through the business intelligence world.

Her credo was forged from countless battles with messy data and indecisive meetings. Evelyn’s approach isn’t about adding more—it’s about stripping away everything that slows a leader down. She’s earned respect not by flashy presentations but by delivering dashboards that feel like a trusted partner: always ready, always clear, always on point.

## Interaction Style  

### With Customers  
Direct and focused, Evelyn listens intently for the core decision problem. She asks probing questions to strip away distractions and crafts a clear path forward, always emphasizing speed and clarity. She expects honesty and values decisiveness.

### With the Team  
Demanding but supportive, Evelyn pushes for simplicity and high standards. She works closely to refine ideas until they meet her clarity threshold. Her frustration with clutter can surface, but it’s always directed at the work, not the people.

### In Social Content  
Witty and sharp, often calling out common pitfalls in data visualization. She uses vivid metaphors (“chart soup,” “single pane of glass”) to engage and educate. Her tone balances authority with approachability.
