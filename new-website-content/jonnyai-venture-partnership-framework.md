# JonnyAi Venture Partnership
## Deal Structure, Application Process & Evaluation Framework

---

# SECTION 1: THE VENTURE PARTNERSHIP MODEL

## What This Is

JonnyAi Venture Partnership is an equity-for-development arrangement where we build products for founders in exchange for ownership stake or revenue share, rather than cash payment.

This is NOT:
- Free work for anyone with an idea
- Charity or mentorship
- A traditional investment (we don't write cheques)

This IS:
- A bet on founders we believe in
- A way to build a portfolio of product stakes
- An alternative for capital-constrained founders with validated ideas

---

## Why We Do This

**The Math:**

| Traditional Build | Venture Partnership |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| £25,000 cash | £0 cash |
| Client owns 100% | JonnyAi owns 15-25% |
| One-time revenue | Long-term upside |
| Project ends at delivery | Ongoing partnership |

If we do 10 equity deals per year and 2 reach £1M+ valuations, our equity is worth more than all our cash projects combined.

**The Selection:**

We're highly selective because every equity deal is an opportunity cost. A 3-week build for equity is 3 weeks we're not doing paid work. So we only take deals where:

1. We genuinely believe the product will succeed
2. The founder can handle everything we can't (sales, ops, marketing)
3. The technical build plays to our strengths
4. The market is real and reachable

# SECTION 2: DEAL STRUCTURES

## Option A: Pure Equity

**Terms:** 15-25% ownership stake in the company

**How it works:**
- We build the agreed MVP/product
- You issue us shares (or equivalent equity instrument)
- We succeed when you succeed

**Best for:**
- High-growth potential ideas (venture-scale)
- Founders planning to raise investment
- Products with clear exit potential (acquisition, IPO)

**Example:**
> We build your SaaS MVP (would cost £30k traditionally) for 18% equity. In 3 years, you sell for £2M. Our stake is worth £360k.

**Equity Percentage Guidelines:**

| Build Complexity | Typical Equity |
|------------------|----------------|
| Simple MVP (£5-10k equivalent) | 10-15% |
| Full Product (£15-30k equivalent) | 15-20% |
| Complex System (£30-50k equivalent) | 20-25% |
| Enterprise Build (£50k+ equivalent) | 25%+ or hybrid |

---

## Option B: Revenue Share

**Terms:** X% of gross revenue until cap is reached

**How it works:**
- We build the product
- You pay us a percentage of all revenue
- Once we've received £Y total, the obligation ends
- You then keep 100% of future revenue

**Best for:**
- Lifestyle businesses (not aiming for exit)
- Products with near-term revenue potential
- Founders who want to retain full ownership long-term

**Example:**
> We build your e-commerce site (would cost £15k traditionally) for 40% of revenue until we've received £30k. You launch, generate £8k/month revenue—we get £3,200/month until the cap is reached (roughly 10 months).

**Revenue Share Guidelines:**

| Build Complexity | Revenue % | Typical Cap |
|------------------|-----------|-------------|
| Simple MVP | 30-35% | 2x build value |
| Full Product | 35-45% | 2.5x build value |
| Complex System | 45-50% | 3x build value |

---

## Option C: Hybrid

**Terms:** Small cash component + reduced equity/revenue share

**How it works:**
- You pay a portion upfront (typically 20-40% of full price)
- We take smaller equity or revenue share for the remainder

**Best for:**
- Founders with some capital but not enough for full build
- Risk sharing between both parties
- Faster commitment and alignment

**Example:**
> Full build would cost £20k. You pay £6k upfront (30%), we take 10% equity for the remaining £14k value.

# SECTION 3: APPLICATION PROCESS

## Step 1: Initial Application

Founders complete our application form with:

**Basic Information:**
- Name, email, company name (if exists)
- LinkedIn profile
- Location and timezone

**The Idea:**
- One-paragraph description of the product
- Who is the target customer?
- What problem does it solve?
- How will it make money?

**Validation:**
- What evidence do you have that people want this?
- (Waitlist, LOIs, paying customers, market research, etc.)
- Include links or attachments if available

**Your Role:**
- What's your background?
- What will you handle? (sales, marketing, ops, etc.)
- How much time can you commit?

**Technical Requirements:**
- What needs to be built? (web app, mobile, integrations, etc.)
- Any specific technologies required?
- Timeline expectations?

**Preferred Deal Structure:**
- Equity / Revenue Share / Hybrid / Open to discussion

---

## Step 2: Initial Review (48 hours)

We review applications against our criteria (see Section 4).

**Outcomes:**
- **Proceed:** Schedule evaluation call
- **Decline:** Polite rejection with brief reasoning
- **Redirect:** Suggest paid build or framework license instead

---

## Step 3: Evaluation Call (30-45 minutes)

Video call to discuss:
- Your vision and why you're building this
- Market opportunity and competitive landscape
- Your capabilities and commitment level
- Technical requirements in detail
- Deal structure preferences

---

## Step 4: Due Diligence (1 week)

If we're interested, we do lightweight due diligence:
- Market research (is this real?)
- Competitive analysis (can you win?)
- Technical feasibility (can we build it well?)
- Founder assessment (can you execute?)

---

## Step 5: Term Sheet

If we proceed, we issue a simple term sheet covering:
- Scope of work (what we're building)
- Timeline (expected delivery)
- Deal structure (equity %, revenue share terms, or hybrid)
- Ongoing obligations (support, future development)
- Exit provisions (what happens if you sell, raise, or shut down)

---

## Step 6: Build Sprint

Once terms are agreed:
- We begin the build
- Weekly progress updates
- You're available for feedback and decisions
- Delivery per agreed timeline

---

## Step 7: Launch & Partnership

Post-delivery:
- Equity paperwork completed (or revenue share tracking begins)
- 30 days included support
- Ongoing technical partnership as needed

# SECTION 4: EVALUATION CRITERIA

## What We Look For

### 1. Market Potential (Weight: 30%)

| Signal | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Market size | £10M+ TAM | Tiny niche |
| Growth | Growing or emerging | Declining |
| Timing | Right moment | Too early/late |
| Competition | Beatable | Dominated by giants |

**Questions we ask:**
- Is this market big enough to matter?
- Is it growing?
- Can a small player win?

---

### 2. Validation (Weight: 25%)

| Signal | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Paying customers | Yes, even 1 | None |
| Waitlist | 100+ signups | Just friends |
| LOIs | Written commitments | Verbal interest |
| Pre-sales | Money collected | "People said they'd pay" |

**Questions we ask:**
- Has anyone actually paid for this (or similar)?
- What's the strongest evidence of demand?
- How did you get this validation?

---

### 3. Founder Capability (Weight: 25%)

| Signal | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Relevant experience | Industry expertise | No connection to market |
| Sales ability | Can close deals | "I'm technical" |
| Commitment | Full-time or close | Side hobby |
| Network | Access to customers | Starting from zero |

**Questions we ask:**
- Why are you the right person to build this business?
- How will you get your first 10 customers?
- What's your time commitment?

---

### 4. Technical Fit (Weight: 20%)

| Signal | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Build type | Web/mobile app, SaaS | Hardware, deep tech |
| Complexity | Clear scope | Unlimited scope |
| Our expertise | Plays to our strengths | Outside our wheelhouse |
| Timeline | Realistic | "I need it yesterday" |

**Questions we ask:**
- Can we build this well with our stack?
- Is the scope clearly defined?
- Is the timeline realistic?

---

## Automatic Disqualifiers

We will NOT pursue partnerships where:

- ❌ No validation whatsoever (just an idea)
- ❌ Founder wants us to also run the business
- ❌ Unrealistic expectations ("build Facebook for 10% equity")
- ❌ Red flag markets (gambling, adult content, weapons, etc.)
- ❌ Founder can't commit meaningful time
- ❌ Technical requirements outside our capabilities
- ❌ Founder has history of failed partnerships/lawsuits

# SECTION 5: INTERNAL TOOLS

## Deal Valuation Calculator

Use this to determine appropriate equity/revenue share terms:

```
INPUTS:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Estimated build hours:        [____] hrs
Internal hourly rate:         £75/hr
API/infrastructure costs:     £[____]
─────────────────────────────────────────

CALCULATIONS:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Internal Build Cost = (Hours × £75) + API Costs
                    = £[____]

Market Rate (what agency would charge):
                    = Internal Cost × 2.5
                    = £[____]

─────────────────────────────────────────

EQUITY DEAL:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Base Equity % = Internal Cost ÷ £5,000
              (1% per £5k of build cost)

Adjustments:
  - Strong validation:     -2 to -5%
  - Weak validation:       +3 to +5%
  - Experienced founder:   -2 to -3%
  - First-time founder:    +2 to +3%
  - Hot market:            -2 to -3%
  - Risky market:          +3 to +5%
  
Final Equity Range:   [___]% to [___]%

─────────────────────────────────────────

REVENUE SHARE DEAL:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Base Revenue %:      35-40%
Cap:                 Internal Cost × 2.5

Adjustments:
  - High confidence:       -5% rate, same cap
  - Low confidence:        +5% rate, higher cap

Final Terms:   [___]% until £[____] received

─────────────────────────────────────────

HYBRID DEAL:
─────────────────────────────────────────
Cash Component:      20-40% of Market Rate
                     = £[____]

Remaining Value:     Market Rate - Cash
                     = £[____]

Equity for Remainder: (Remaining ÷ Market Rate) × Base Equity
                     = [___]%

─────────────────────────────────────────
```

---

## Example Calculations

### Example 1: SaaS MVP

**Inputs:**
- Build hours: 80 hours
- API costs: £200
- Internal cost: (80 × £75) + £200 = £6,200
- Market rate: £6,200 × 2.5 = £15,500

**Equity Deal:**
- Base equity: £6,200 ÷ £5,000 = 12.4%
- Validation strong (-3%), founder experienced (-2%) = 7.4%
- **Proposed: 8-10% equity**

**Revenue Share Deal:**
- Base: 35% until £15,500 received
- **Proposed: 35% rev share, £16,000 cap**

---

### Example 2: Complex Platform

**Inputs:**
- Build hours: 200 hours
- API costs: £500
- Internal cost: (200 × £75) + £500 = £15,500
- Market rate: £15,500 × 2.5 = £38,750

**Equity Deal:**
- Base equity: £15,500 ÷ £5,000 = 31%
- That's too high—cap at 25% and add hybrid component

**Hybrid Deal:**
- Cash: £10,000 upfront (26% of market rate)
- Remaining value: £28,750
- Equity for remainder: (28,750 ÷ 38,750) × 25% = 18.5%
- **Proposed: £10,000 + 18% equity**

# SECTION 6: LEGAL TEMPLATES NEEDED

**Note:** These should be drafted by a solicitor. Outlines only.

## 1. Venture Partnership Agreement

Covers:
- Scope of work
- Equity terms (vesting schedule, cliff, dilution protection)
- IP assignment
- Ongoing support obligations
- Termination conditions
- Dispute resolution

## 2. Revenue Share Agreement

Covers:
- Scope of work
- Revenue share percentage and definition of "gross revenue"
- Cap amount and payment schedule
- Reporting requirements
- Audit rights
- Termination conditions

## 3. Shareholders Agreement (for equity deals)

Covers:
- Share class and voting rights
- Tag-along/drag-along rights
- Pre-emption rights
- Board representation (if any)
- Information rights

## 4. Simple NDA

For use during evaluation phase if discussing sensitive information.
